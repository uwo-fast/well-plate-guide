**Issuing Body:** Society for Laboratory Automation and Screening (SLAS), formerly Society for Biomolecular Screening (SBS)  
**Secretariat:** SLAS, 100 Illinois Street, Ste 242, St. Charles, Illinois 60174  
**Drawing Standard:** ASME Y14.5M-1994, Dimensioning and Tolerancing  
**Reference Temperature:** All dimensions applicable at 20°C (68°F) unless otherwise noted

## Background and History

Microplate dimensional standards originated from a need identified at the first Society for Biomolecular Screening (SBS) meeting in 1995. At that time, microplates from different manufacturers — and even within a single vendor's product line — varied significantly in dimensions, causing frequent problems when plates were used in automated laboratory instrumentation and robotics.

Work began in late 1995 to define standards for the 96-well microplate. The first written proposal was released in December 1995 and circulated through scientific conferences and journals in 1996. The proposed standard was formally presented to SBS membership in October 1996 in Basel, Switzerland. By 1999, formalization efforts were underway; a working committee ListServ was established in March 1999, and quarterly committee meetings began in August 1999. The committee grew to include representatives from over 100 corporations, educational institutions, and government organizations across more than 15 nations.

In July 2010, SBS merged with the Association for Laboratory Automation (ALA) to form the **Society for Laboratory Automation and Screening (SLAS)**. Standards formerly designated ANSI/SBS were re-designated ANSI/SLAS.

## ANSI SLAS 1-2004 (R2012) — Footprint Dimensions
*(formerly ANSI/SBS 1-2004)*

### Scope and Purpose

Defines the dimensional requirements of the **external footprint** (base perimeter) of a microplate. Establishes the minimum dimensions required for standards compliance and conditions for measurement.

### 4.1 Footprint Dimensions

#### 4.1.1.1 Corner Zone Tolerance (within 12.7 mm / 0.5000 in of each outside corner)

| Dimension | Millimeters | Inches |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 127.76 ± 0.25 mm | 5.0299 ± 0.0098 in |
| Width | 85.48 ± 0.25 mm | 3.3654 ± 0.0098 in |

#### 4.1.1.2 Overall Tolerance (measured at any point along the side)

| Dimension | Millimeters | Inches |
|---|---|---|
| Length | 127.76 ± 0.50 mm | 5.0299 ± 0.0197 in |
| Width | 85.48 ± 0.50 mm | 3.3654 ± 0.0197 in |

#### 4.1.1.3 Continuity Requirement

The footprint must be **continuous and uninterrupted** around the entire base of the plate.

### 4.1.2 Corner Radius

The four outside corners of the plate's bottom flange shall have a corner radius (measured to the outside) of:

| Value | Millimeters | Inches |
|---|---|---|
| Corner radius | 3.18 ± 1.60 mm | 0.1252 ± 0.0630 in |

## ANSI SLAS 2-2004 (R2012) — Height Dimensions
*(formerly ANSI/SBS 2-2004)*

### Scope and Purpose

Defines the dimensional requirements of **microplate height**. A plate may comply with either section 4.1 (typical height with clearance) or section 4.2 (typical height). Plates or instruments advertised as compliant must clearly state which part they meet.

### 4.1 Typical Height with Clearance

#### 4.1.1 Plate Height

| Measurement | Millimeters | Inches |
|---|---|---|
| Height to maximum protrusion of perimeter wells (from Datum A) | 14.35 ± 0.25 mm | 0.5650 ± 0.0098 in |
| Overall plate height — maximum protrusion of plate (from Datum A) | 14.35 ± 0.76 mm | 0.5650 ± 0.0299 in |

> **Datum A** is defined as the resting plane — the flat surface on which the plate sits.

#### 4.1.2 Top Surface

- Maximum allowable projection above the **top-stacking surface**: **0.76 mm (0.0299 in)**
  - The top-stacking surface is the surface on which another plate rests when stacked
- When resting on a flat surface, the plate's top surface must be **parallel to the resting surface within 0.76 mm (0.0299 in)**

#### 4.1.3 External Clearance to the Plate Bottom

- Minimum clearance from Datum A to the **external bottom surface of the wells**: **1.00 mm (0.0394 in)**
- This clearance requirement applies **only in the area of the wells**

### 4.2 Typical Height (without clearance requirement)

#### 4.2.1 Plate Height

Identical to 4.1.1:

| Measurement | Millimeters | Inches |
|---|---|---|
| Height to max protrusion of perimeter wells (from Datum A) | 14.35 ± 0.25 mm | 0.5650 ± 0.0098 in |
| Overall plate height — max protrusion (from Datum A) | 14.35 ± 0.76 mm | 0.5650 ± 0.0299 in |

#### 4.2.2 Top Surface

Identical to 4.1.2:

- Maximum projection above top-stacking surface: **0.76 mm (0.0299 in)**
- Top surface parallelism to resting surface: within **0.76 mm (0.0299 in)**

> **Note:** Section 4.2 does not include the 1 mm external clearance requirement of 4.1.3. Compliance with 4.1 vs 4.2 must be explicitly declared.

|---|---|---|
| 96 | 9.00 mm (0.3543 in) | 4.50 mm (0.1772 in) |
| 384 | 4.50 mm (0.1772 in) | 2.25 mm (0.0886 in) |
| 1536 | 2.25 mm (0.0886 in) | 1.125 mm (0.0443 in) |

### Figure Notes (SLAS 2)

## ANSI SLAS 3-2004 (R2012) — Bottom Outside Flange Dimensions
*(formerly ANSI/SBS 3-2004)*

### Scope and Purpose

Defines the dimensional requirements of the **bottom outside flange** of a microplate. Plates may comply with any one of five flange configurations (4.1 through 4.5). Compliance must be explicitly stated.

### General Flange Rules (all variants)

- Flange width measured at the top of the flange: **minimum 1.27 mm (0.0500 in)** on all sides
- Chamfers (corner notches) are optional in quantity and location, but **must not include the flange**
- All measurements from Datum A (bottom resting plane) to top of flange

## ANSI SLAS 4-2004 (R2012) — Well Positions
*(formerly ANSI/SBS 4-2004)*

### Scope and Purpose

Defines the **well center positional requirements** of a microplate for 96-well, 384-well, and 1536-well formats. Plates may comply with any one of the three formats; compliance must be explicitly stated.

### General Notes

- The **left edge** of the plate for column position measurement is defined as the two 12.7 mm zones at the corners, as specified in SLAS 1
- The **top edge** for row position measurement is similarly defined
- Positional tolerance is specified as **True Position** (circular tolerance zone), applying at RFS (regardless of feature size)
- The **top-left well** must be clearly marked (e.g., letter "A" or numeral "1" on the left side, or numeral "1" on the upper side). Additional markings are permitted.

## ANSI SLAS 6-2012 — Well Bottom Elevation
*(formerly SBS-6)*

### Scope and Purpose

Defines **well bottom elevation** for microplates with flat well bottoms. Establishes definitions, measurement conditions, equipment requirements, and test procedures. This standard specifies definitions and a test method **only** — it does not set numeric pass/fail limits for WBE, WBEV, or IWBEV. Limits should be specified by instrument manufacturers relative to these protocols.

**References:** ASME Y14.5M-1994; ANSI/SLAS 2-2004; ANSI/SLAS 4-2004

### Definitions

| Term | Abbreviation | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Well Bottom Elevation | WBE | Distance from Datum A to the inside bottom surface of any well, measured at the SBS/SLAS well location coordinates. Reported as nominal ± tolerance. |
| Well Bottom Elevation Variation | WBEV | Maximum allowable spread between the highest (WBE/MAX) and lowest (WBE/MIN) WBE points on an individual plate. Reported as a maximum value. |
| Intra-Well Bottom Elevation Variation | IWBEV | Range (max − min) of the distance from Datum A to anywhere on the inside bottom surface of any single individual well. Reported as a maximum value. |
| Well Depth | — | Distance from the maximum projection of any individual well to anywhere on the inside bottom surface of that well. Reported as nominal ± tolerance. |
| Bottom Thickness | — | Mean thickness of all well bottoms on any individual plate. Reported as a nominal value. |
| Well Bottom Width | — | Internal dimension (width or diameter) of any individual flat bottom well, measured at the inside bottom surface to the theoretical sharp corner (tangency between bottom surface and sidewalls). Reported as nominal. |
| Max Projection | — | As defined in ANSI/SLAS 2-2004. |
| Plate Height | — | As defined in ANSI/SLAS 2-2004. |
| Minimum External Clearance to Plate Bottom | — | As defined in ANSI/SLAS 2-2004. |

> **Datum A** = the resting plane (bottom of the plate as it sits on a flat surface).

### 4 Equipment

Any combination of measuring components capable of measuring the required distances within stated accuracy and precision, **without causing significant displacement or distortion** of the microplate. Acceptable equipment includes a Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM) with fixture, or a custom test apparatus.

### 5 Test Setup Requirements

#### 5.1 Repeatability, Reproducibility, and Accuracy
- Repeatability and Reproducibility of the test setup must be established
- Accuracy established via calibration of individual measuring tools or the complete system

#### 5.2 Temperature
- Parts and equipment must be conditioned to test temperature until stable
- Test must be conducted at **25°C ± 2°C**

#### 5.3 Part History
- Parts must be in the as-manufactured condition as packaged for delivery, with no prior heat cycling or other history

#### 5.4 Force and Displacement
- The test setup must not distort or displace the plate in any significant way
- **Significance:** displacement greater than 10% of the part tolerance

#### 5.5 Alignment
- Measurement of well bottom surfaces must be **perpendicular to Datum A**

### 6 Test Procedure

**Objective:** Determine Well Bottom Elevation data by measuring the distance from Datum A to the inside bottom surface of each well.

#### 6.1 Measurement Steps
1. Position the plate with Datum A (resting plane) facing down. **Do not constrain the part flat to Datum A.**
2. Establish datums B-C and D-E
3. Set zero-zero-zero at datum intersections
4. Establish position based on well coordinates per ANSI/SLAS 4
5. Measure and record the distance from Datum A to the inside bottom surface of each well
6. Repeat for every well

#### 6.2 Values to Record

| Value | Definition |
|---|---|
| WBE | Average value of all wells on the plate |
| WBE/MAX | Maximum individual well WBE value across all plates |
| WBE/MIN | Minimum individual well WBE value across all plates |
| WBEV | Range of all well WBE values on each plate (WBE/MAX − WBE/MIN per plate) |
| IWBE/MAX | Maximum value measured within each individual well |
| IWBE/MIN | Minimum value measured within each individual well |
| IWBEV | Range within each well (IWBE/MAX − IWBE/MIN) |

### 7 Limits

This standard specifies **definitions and a test method only**. No numeric limits are stated for WBE, WBEV, or IWBEV. Instrument manufacturers are expected to specify their own requirements for these values with reference to these protocols.

## Annex A: Interpretation of Figures (All Standards)

All five standards include an informative Annex A providing guidance for interpreting engineering drawings in accordance with ASME Y14.5M-1994. Annex A is **not part of the normative standard** and does not substitute for knowledge of ASME dimensioning and tolerancing practices.

### A.1 — Specifying Datum
Shows how datum reference frames (Datum A as resting plane, Datum B-C and D-E as side references) are constructed from the plate geometry in accordance with ASME Y14.5M-1994.

### A.2 — Specifying Composite Datum
Illustrates composite datum application using the four 12.7 mm corner zones of the plate (zones B-C and D-E), establishing the reference frame for footprint and well position measurements.

### A.3 — Application of Composite Datum
Demonstrates how composite datums constrain the plate in a three-plane datum reference frame (primary Datum A = bottom resting plane; secondary Datum B-C = long side corners; tertiary Datum D-E = short side corners).

### A.4 — Parallelism
Illustrates parallelism callouts per ASME Y14.5M-1994 §6.6.3.1. The controlled surface must lie within a 0.76 mm (0.0299 in) wide tolerance zone, parallel to Datum A, and must not exceed limits of size.

### A.5 — Tolerances over a Limited Area
Illustrates the two-tier tolerance scheme used throughout these standards: a tighter tolerance (e.g., ±0.25 mm) applies within a restricted zone (such as the corner areas or Area "K"), while a broader tolerance (e.g., ±0.76 mm) applies to the whole surface.

### A.6 — True Position vs ± Tolerance (SLAS 4 only)
Explains the geometric advantage of circular True Position tolerancing over square ±tolerance zones. A ±0.25 mm square tolerance zone has an area of 0.25 × 0.25 × 4 = 0.25 mm²; a circular True Position zone of Ø 0.71 mm has an area of ~0.393 mm², which is approximately 57% more area while still constraining the feature within the same worst-case envelope.
